Discussions: Talk Amongst Yourselves

LittleRedBrick's discussion groups are a great communication tool. They are essentially collections of internet forums (if that doesn't mean anything to you, think of an organized, permanent version of email list discussions that don't clutter up your mailbox). Here's how they work:

Everyone who can access your school 's LittleRedBrick site is automatically a member of certain groups. Everyone is a member of the school group; there is a group for staff only, one for staff and parents, one just for teachers, and every class gets a discussion group too. As an administrator, you can write out announcements or topics you want a group to discuss, and post them for everyone in the group to read and respond. Only group members can see the topics and responses in a discussion group. You and your staff can use discussion groups for anything you want to talk about -

Staff Meetings

Post agendas. Talk about issues raised in meetings. Tell argumentative teachers to take it up online so the rest of you can keep your meeting on schedule.

Union Business

Teachers can campaign for union positions. Union reps can report back to the staff about union meetings.

Extracurriculars

Have a staff forum where coaches can discuss roles. Use a school forum to communicate eligibility standards and expectations for student behavior.

Class Discussion

Teachers can start topics on particular assignments, test review, book discussions, post class rules, and anything else they can imagine.

Your staff can also access a tech support discussion group, where you can find answers to questions about LittleRedBrick, suggest new features you would like us to add, and share tips with other users about what you are doing with the site.
